Lamayuru, built in 11th century, is also home to another of Ladakh's ancient and secluded monk School. Set in a surreal surrounding, it is called the Moonland because of its topography which reminds one of the surface of the moon. As special invitees to the morning prayer in the Lamayuru monastery, we were witness to an incredibly uplifting spiritual experience. After a quick walk around the town, we set off on our way, to reach our first campsite of the trek before loss of daylight.

Bouncing and skipping our way through the stony river beds and tricky trails, we were ushered into a sunny day of fun trekking. Our halt for the day was at Sumdo Chenmo, a village which can only be reached after 2 days of trek. Our team decided to light up the village by offering Solar Lanterns to them. A village tucked in the darkness of Zanskar ranges got its first glimpse of light. Separated from any electricity lines, these lamps would ensure the villagers have light in the night.

After a rejuvenating stay in the warm quarters offered to us by the villagers of Sumda Chenmo, we set out on our last day of trekking, the one which I like to call the "Stream crossing day". Surrounded by colourful mountains, we waded through, jumped over, or walked over a bridge across at least 2 dozen streams, small and big. We camped the night at Chilling, a stone's throw away from the Indus river.
After a rejuvenating stay in the warm quarters offered to us by the villagers of Sumda Chenmo, we set out on our last day of trekking, the one which I like to call the "Stream crossing day". Surrounded by colourful mountains, we waded through, jumped over, or walked over a bridge across at least 2 dozen streams, small and big. We camped the night at Chilling, a stone's throw away from the Indus river.
The Last leg of adventure was white water rafting. Squeezing ourselves into our wetsuits and arming ourselves with colorful oars, we broke up into groups that would mount the rafts kept ready for us. Guided by experts, and steered by our own mad adrenaline rush, we flowed along with the river, through the rough patches and the calm ones in the Zanskar river.
Back to Leh, we set out for Khardungla, the pass which boasts of the highest motorable road in the world. It was a quick drive up the mountains, and we could feel the rising altitude by the buzz in our ears. After having some hot tea and snacks at Khardungla, and clicking quite a few snaps for the instagram album, we headed back to Leh, were we then attended the city's first TEDx event. We headed out to the city market soon after, for some last minute souvenir shopping, while trying hard to reconcile with the fact that the next day would have us all flying off in different directions. If only great times could last forever

Testimonials

Jaideep Bansal, Manager, Proctor & Gamble

There are moments in life which make you realize that there is much more in you as an individual than what you are currently. I have always heard such stories where people said that certain experiences in life changed them. Now, through GHE, I have lived such a story. Since coming back from GHE, I am running marathons, going on cycling expeditions, volunteering for causes; in the process discovering a new side of me, which I am really enjoying.

Ankur Ahuja, Associate Consultant at Bain & Company

GHE was the experience of a lifetime. To be able to meet such interesting and well-rounded people from all across the globe - from the CEO of the biggest solar power company in India to people who had travelled from the North Pole to the South Pole on bamboo bikes, in a place as stunning as Ladakh, while setting up a fully functioning e-base running 100% on solar power, all in one trip - crazy! I went alone, I came back with amazing memories, great friends and buckets full of inspiring ideas. I went a sceptic, I came back a believer.

Ilesha Kharbanda, Strategy Consultant at Grant Thornton India LLP

Everything I had went through in the last few days has been all firsts - first time trekking in the Himalayas, first time camping, first time cycling through the mountains. I can’t believe I am here, having finished all this, and I am actually now ready to go on again.

Lokesh Todi, Yale MBA Candidate 2014

I had come with no expectations at all, had come just to see Leh, meet some great people. But the impact that I have seen GHE make is profound, from setting up the Education Base and seeing the children smile when they were using the tablets and the internet for the first time, to going to one of the villages and distributing solar lanterns, where they have never had electricity before. These things have made a big difference in my life...
